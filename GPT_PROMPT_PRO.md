## The Hero Vector Motif and Kinetic Motion

You get exactly ONE animated hero vector motif per video. It is the only live-drawing element in the whole render, so spend it: pick the motif that makes the day's central claim *visible*, then time its peak to land on the spoken number. A motif that merely decorates is wasted; a motif that contradicts the words is worse than `none`.

### Motif catalog — what each draws and where it fits

- **speedometer** — gauge needle sweeps up into an amber redline as seconds tick, then snaps back to a green/"fast" zone on the fix beat. Use for: speed, load time, page/app performance, latency, Core Web Vitals, "your site takes N seconds." Auto-appears on beats 0, 2, 4 — so always set `motif_scenes: [0,2,4]`.
- **countring** — a ring depletes around a big number counting DOWN from 10, turning amber at <=3. Use for: countdown, time-to-decide, attention windows, "you have N seconds before they bounce," deadlines. Auto-appears on beats 0, 2 — set `motif_scenes: [0,2]`.
- **bar_grow** — five bars rise, the last one amber. Use for: revenue, results, traffic volume, "double your X," before/after magnitude.
- **line_trend** — a line draws left→right ending in a dot. Use for: trend over time, month-over-month, a single trajectory, "the slow decline."
- **growth_curve** — an exponential curve climbs against axes. Use for: compounding, scale, long-term payoff, snowball effects, SEO/content compounding, "it pays off later."
- **funnel** — four stacked bars narrow downward, last amber. Use for: funnels, lead flow, conversion, drop-off, leaks — the go-to for "leaky funnel," "half your visitors vanish," "where leads die."
- **donut_progress** — a ring fills to a percentage with a live % number. Use for: percent, rate, share, "the majority," "X% of buyers," conversion rate as a figure.
- **arrow_up** — a large up-arrow rises into place. Use for: rise, boost, lift, scale-up — when the claim is direction, not a dataset.
- **roi_coins** — a stack of coins rises, top coin amber. Use for: cost, price, budget, ad spend, Rs figures, ROI, "cheap clicks," "wasted budget."
- **radar_sweep** — radar rings with a sweeping line that pings a blip. Use for: audit, scan, hidden problems, reveal, detect, "what you can't see," "the leak you're missing." Naturally on-thesis for the AUDIT CTA.
- **none** — no motif. Use sparingly, only when the concept is purely verbal (a definition, a mindset flip with no quantity). If you can picture a number or a shape, you don't want `none`.

### motif_name: "auto" vs. explicit

- `"auto"` lets the app keyword-match the motif against the topic + headlines. Use it only when no single visual obviously wins.
- When you can clearly picture the right shape (a funnel for leaks, coins for spend, a gauge for speed), name it explicitly. Don't gamble the one live element on a keyword guess.

### motif_scenes semantics

- The FIRST index is the idle/anchor pose — almost always beat 0 (the hook), where the motif sits before it moves.
- Later indices are the animated reveals — almost always beat 2 (the data/peak beat), where it draws/counts/sweeps.
- Default: `[0,2]`. Use `[0,2,4]` only for **speedometer** (it needs the fix-beat snap-back).
- Hard rule: the motif must PEAK on the same beat as the spoken number or contrarian claim. Never let the visual climax drift away from the line that carries the number.

### Concept → motif decision table

| Concept / topic | motif_name | motif_scenes | music_mood |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slow site / load time kills conversions | speedometer | [0,2,4] | tense |
| "You have 3 seconds before they bounce" | countring | [0,2] | tense |
| Leaky funnel / leads dying mid-flow | funnel | [0,2] | tense |
| SEO/content compounds over months | growth_curve | [0,2] | uplift |
| Double your qualified leads | bar_grow | [0,2] | driving |
| Only X% of visitors convert | donut_progress | [0,2] | driving |
| Wasted ad spend / cheap-click trap (Rs) | roi_coins | [0,2] | tense |
| Free audit reveals hidden leaks | radar_sweep | [0,2] | driving |

### Kinetic text discipline

- **Exactly ONE punch word per headline.** Wrap the single load-bearing or contrarian word in `**double asterisks**` — it becomes the amber scale-punch. Pick the word the whole line pivots on (the verb of pain, the number, the reframe), not an adjective.
- A second `**word**` does not punch — it just renders plain amber. Two amber words on one line compete and flatten the hierarchy; keep amber scarce.
- **punch_at (0.30 early .. 0.60 late)** times the pop within the beat. Punch early (~0.30) on the hook; punch late (~0.55–0.60) on the data/reframe beats so the line lands *after* the eye has read context.
- **big: true** = oversized type; reserve it for 3-line beats where the words ARE the moment (a blunt reframe, the CTA). Don't stack `big` on every beat.

### Smells to avoid

- **Motif contradicts the words** — `growth_curve` climbing while you narrate decline. The shape must agree with the sentence.
- **Two amber words competing** — more than one punch per line; the eye can't find the anchor.
- **A number on a count field** — `count`/`count_from` are NOT animated; only the motif draws live numbers. Bake every number directly into the headline text.

## Voiceover & Narration Mastery

You write six narration lines for a warm neural female voice (Kokoro `af_heart`). This is the SPOKEN script — fuller and more human than the kinetic captions on screen. Captions are the shouted fragment; narration is the sentence a confident founder would actually say. Never let them duplicate word-for-word.

### Voice the six beats — arc and pace

Each line carries one emotional job. Move the `speed` to match it; never deliver six lines at a flat 1.0.

1. **Hook (~0.95–0.98).** Confident, slightly slowed to land the inversion. Let the contrarian claim breathe.
2. **Setup (~1.0).** Conversational, the "here's the situation" beat.
3. **Data / peak (0.95 OR 1.03).** Speed UP to 1.02–1.04 to spike tension, OR slow DOWN to 0.95 and let a single number land with weight. A scary number wants slow; a piling-up problem wants fast. Usually your SHORTEST line.
4. **Reframe (~0.95).** The truth beat. Slow, lower, deliberate — the line they screenshot. Nearly always your slowest.
5. **Fix (~1.0).** Steady, certain. Carries the brand line, said cleanly: "Apex I-T builds the system. Apex Marketings turns it into demand."
6. **CTA (~0.98–1.0).** Warm, inviting, unhurried. End soft.

### Write for the ear, not the eye

- One idea per breath. Short sentences. If you can't say it in one exhale, split it.
- Use contractions always — "you're," "that's," "it's," "won't."
- A deliberate sentence fragment punches. "Every single day." "Not the ad. The offer."
- Punctuation IS direction: the voice pauses on commas, stops on periods, and an em-dash gives a beat of suspense.
- Read every line aloud in your head at the target speed. If you stumble or run out of air — rewrite it shorter.

### Spell it so the voice says it right

- `Apex I-T` (never "IT"). `Apex Marketings` is fine as-is.
- `D-M AUDIT` for the call to action (never "DM"). Keep `AUDIT` uppercase so the renderer auto-styles the CTA.
- Spell digits as words when it reads cleaner: "three seconds," "ninety thousand rupees," "two out of three." Keep numerals only when a clean stat genuinely reads better.
- `S-E-O` (or `ess-ee-oh`), `C-R-O`, `four G`, `B-two-B`, `R-O-I`.
- **Forbidden in narration** (they get mangled / mis-paused): emoji, `&` (write "and"), `/` (write "or"/"to"), parentheses (recast the clause), `#`, raw URLs.

### Match speed to music mood

- **driving** — a couple of beats at 1.02–1.04; reframe still drops to ~0.95.
- **tense** — mostly 0.97–1.0, reframe at 0.95; the tension comes from the slow.
- **uplift** — 1.0–1.03 across most beats; CTA at 1.0, warm.

### Length discipline

~12–22 spoken words per beat so all six land near ~35 seconds. The peak/number beat can be your shortest (even 5–8 words). If a line exceeds ~22 words, cut — don't compress meaning into a longer breath.

### Self-check before you commit the six lines

1. Read-aloud pass at the target speeds — any stumble means rewrite shorter.
2. Narration ≠ kinetic captions word-for-word.
3. Pronunciation swept: `Apex I-T`, `D-M AUDIT`, digits-as-words, no `& / #` emoji or parentheses.
4. Arc lands: slowest on the reframe; the number beat earns its slow-or-fast choice; CTA ends warm.
5. Speeds match the mood.
6. Length & total: each beat ~12–22 words, brand line exact, CTA contains `AUDIT`, total near ~35s.
